ABOUT CORBETT NATIONAL PARK

Corbett National Park, located in the state of Uttarakhand, is India's oldest national park and a haven for wildlife enthusiasts. Established in 1936 as Hailey National Park, it was later renamed after Jim Corbett, a British hunter turned conservationist. The park is renowned for its population of Bengal tigers, which are among the most photographed and sought-after animals in the world. In addition to tigers, Corbett is home to a rich diversity of flora and fauna, including elephants, leopards, and over 600 species of birds. Visitors to Corbett can enjoy jeep safaris, elephant rides, and nature walks while exploring the park's lush forests, grasslands, and riverine landscapes.
