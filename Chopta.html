ABOUT CHOPTA

Chopta, often referred to as the "Mini Switzerland of Uttarakhand," is a scenic hill station located in the Rudraprayag district of Uttarakhand, India. Situated at an altitude of approximately 2,680 meters (8,790 feet) above sea level, Chopta offers breathtaking views of the surrounding Himalayan peaks, including Nanda Devi, Trishul, and Chaukhamba. The region is known for its lush green meadows, dense forests of rhododendron and deodar trees, and serene ambiance, making it a perfect destination for nature lovers, trekkers, and adventure enthusiasts. Chopta serves as the base camp for the trek to Tungnath Temple, the highest Shiva temple in the world, and offers trekking routes to Chandrashila Peak, which provides panoramic views of the Himalayas. Visitors to Chopta can enjoy activities such as camping, birdwatching, and photography amidst the pristine natural beauty of the region.
