ABOUT VALLEY OF FLOWERS NATIONAL PARK

Valley of Flowers National Park, located in the Indian state of Uttarakhand, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site renowned for its stunning alpine meadows and diverse flora. Spread over an area of 87.50 km2, the park is nestled in the Western Himalayas and is home to numerous species of flowers, including the elusive blue poppy. The park's vibrant colors and breathtaking landscapes attract nature enthusiasts, botanists, and photographers from around the world. Visitors to the Valley of Flowers can embark on scenic treks through the park, exploring its picturesque valleys, gushing streams, and cascading waterfalls.
